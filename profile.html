The Visitor's advance

I had expected the world before him to be disintegrating, had expected there to be rocks, soil and garbage floating out of the ground in the light of his advance, but all I could see was a cluster of shapes, casting barely visibly shadows as they drifted about his stooped and lurching form. It was this, more than anything else, that convinced me that the Visitor was in some way diminished. Perhaps my gamble with the black mud had paid off after all, or perhaps the darkness really was eroding the Visitor’s strength. Whatever the case, I was sure that this boded well for us. Firm ground, capricious though it was, meant that we could run, and though I knew it to be unlikely, I begun to entertain the possibility that we could get away.

It was with an effort that I managed

It was with an effort that I managed to turn the focus of my thoughts to ground beneath my feet. I had slipped several times by then, and as we got further and further from the camp, the going seemed to only be getting more difficult. I had imagined that the wall we had crossed to get here in some way surrounded the camp, and that we would have to cross it if we were to find our way back out into the pitch black world beyond, but we never found it. What happened instead was that the colour slowly faded from the world as we got further and further from the camp, till in the end, the lights of the fires behind us vanished, and we were once again moving through a world black as pitch in all direction. The only difference now, was that we were using the false light of the visitor’s aura, rather than the concentrated presence of the Nameless Earth, to orient ourselves.
